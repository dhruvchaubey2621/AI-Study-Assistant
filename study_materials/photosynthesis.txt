## Photosynthesis: Concise Study Summary

Photosynthesis is the fundamental biochemical process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy in the form of glucose (sugar). This process is vital for life on Earth as it produces the oxygen we breathe and forms the base of most food webs.

**Key Aspects:**

1.  **Overall Equation:**
    6CO₂ (Carbon Dioxide) + 6H₂O (Water) + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ (Glucose) + 6O₂ (Oxygen)

2.  **Location:** Primarily occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, specifically within the thylakoid membranes and the stroma. Chlorophyll, a green pigment, is crucial for absorbing light energy.

3.  **Two Main Stages:**
    *   **Light-Dependent Reactions:**
        *   **Where:** Thylakoid membranes within chloroplasts.
        *   **What:** Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll, exciting electrons. Water molecules are split (photolysis) to replace these electrons, releasing oxygen gas as a byproduct. The energy from excited electrons is used to produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate), which are energy-carrying molecules.
        *   **Inputs:** Light energy, Water (H₂O)
        *   **Outputs:** ATP, NADPH, Oxygen (O₂)
    *   **Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle):**
        *   **Where:** Stroma (the fluid-filled space surrounding the thylakoids) of chloroplasts.
        *   **What:** The ATP and NADPH produced in the light-dependent reactions are used to convert carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the atmosphere into glucose. This process is called carbon fixation.
        *   **Inputs:** Carbon Dioxide (CO₂), ATP, NADPH
        *   **Outputs:** Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆), ADP, NADP⁺

4.  **Importance:** Produces food (glucose) for plants and, indirectly, for most other life forms. Releases oxygen, which is essential for aerobic respiration in many organisms.

5.  **Factors Affecting Rate:** Light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, temperature, and water availability can all influence the rate of photosynthesis.

## Five Flashcards with Answers

**Flashcard 1**
*   **Front:** What is photosynthesis?
*   **Back:** The process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy, water, and carbon dioxide into glucose (sugar) and oxygen.

**Flashcard 2**
*   **Front:** Where in a plant cell do the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis take place?
*   **Back:** In the thylakoid membranes within the chloroplasts.

**Flashcard 3**
*   **Front:** Write the overall balanced chemical equation for photosynthesis.
*   **Back:** 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

**Flashcard 4**
*   **Front:** What two energy-carrying molecules are produced during the light-dependent reactions and then used in the Calvin Cycle?
*   **Back:** ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate).

**Flashcard 5**
*   **Front:** What is the primary pigment responsible for absorbing light energy in most photosynthetic organisms?
*   **Back:** Chlorophyll.
